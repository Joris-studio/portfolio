Analysis proved: the structure was missing altogether.

I started mapping out the main journeys but failed. This platform had grown into a broken construct over time. Still I stuck to the project's brief - whose guardrails did not allow me to work on the structure. I focused on having the user understand how your pension plans' money correlates with your age.

Especially the mobile version was one big pile of spaghetti...

Too much white space, too little clarity on what to do as a user.

The approach

I designed a task-oriented prototype based on progressive disclosure — showing information step by step, only when the user needs it.

We tested the prototype with real users. At the same time, I used it to help the client team see that principles like task focus and information hierarchy aren’t abstract theory — they’re what make complex content understandable.

The outcome

The results were clear: users found the content easier to follow and understood what to do next. The client, initially sceptical, was pleasantly surprised.

It opened up a broader conversation: how could we apply the same structure across the rest of the site? And how could we use data to measure and improve it further?

The impact

The project didn’t just deliver a better prototype. It changed how the client thought about design — structure isn’t decoration, it’s the foundation of a usable platform.
